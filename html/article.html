Process for delivering a project

As you’d expect, all our digital projects follow a clear methodology. This is quite flexible, and you can see the basic framework in the infographic below.

1Overview

It’s important to remember that digital projects require many people to work together. So as well as you, there’ll also be involvement from the legal and compliance department, external agencies, the business, IT and many more.

All digital projects vary in lead times; it can take from a few weeks to a few months to get to launch day. Your DT will be able to give you a rough timeline, so don’t be shy, get in touch with them.

2Ideas

It all starts with an idea. It can be a brainwave you’ve had for using digital to enhance your brand, or you may have received a proposal from your agency. It could even be an initiative that has come out of an internal conversation. Whatever the source, an idea will soon be transformed into a brief.

Read More

3Planning

This is when your idea really starts to take shape – and to transform itself into a plan of action. Your agency should be putting together a response to your brief to them, which includes a concrete articulation of the potential solution, and corresponding estimated cost and time needed to bring the solution to life.

We recommend that you involve your DT in reviewing the response from the agency, as they will be able to help evaluate the plan, the proposed costs and scope of work through a more a technical lens. They’ll also provide useful insights based on their digital experience across other brands.

Read More

4Design and production

You’ll enter this phase once you have approved the costs, the plan and the proposed solution, and the commercial contracts are in place. This phase takes you all the way from early scamps and sketches, to fully annotated wireframes, designs and then through to the production of assets. It includes not just designing the look and feel, but designing and producing content, finalising SEO and analytics requirements.

Read More

5Build, test and launch

Job done! You are now free to go back to your normal job. That’s not to say you don’t need to stay involved, but expect the demands on your time to lessen from this point on – assuming your DT has been plugged into the process all along.

This phase is when the solution that you’ve carefully envisioned and designed will be coded and tested – and brought to life.

Read More

Now that you’ve launched your digital property and are receiving daily updates, stop for a moment and enjoy the feeling of your achievement! But you’re not quite finished yet…
