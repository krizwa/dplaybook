Ideas

It all starts with an idea. It can be a brainwave that the brand team may have had for using digital to enhance their brand, or they may have received a proposal from the agency. It could even be an initative that has come out of internal comms from your Digital Technology team. Whatever the source, an idea will soon be transformed into a brief.

1Prepare the PID document

If this is a completely new initiative for which the brand team needs funding, a PID (Project Initiation Document) will have to be prepared first. This could be the first port of call for a Brand Manager to get in touch with you, seeking advice on how to frame the PID, and also perhaps to draw out a roadmap.

2Look up the agency roster

If the brand team has not yet identified which agency they would want to work on this, then you could help them through the process of agency selection:

  • On request from the brand team, you, the ARM or procurement lead would look up agencies already accredited and on the global digital agency roster. Accredited agencies are trained in the Diageo ways of working and have access to the Horizon gateway.
  • The brand team might want to work with an agency that is not on the global roster. In this case, they should get advice from their demand procurement team.
  • In order to develop and build digital assets for Diageo, agencies need to go through a formal accreditation process:
      1. First, send the agency the digital questionnaire to complete
      2. Then you should score the response
      3. If the agency passes, they will be added to the global roster
      4. If the agency fails, you, Procurement or ARM can suggest a different agency to partner with.

3Create a project brief

Once the agency is in place, you should assist the brand team in preparing a brief. This will help to articulate the idea to the agency, and set it in context so that both you and your agency are in agreement on the project’s requirements, and key parameters such as budget, deadlines, etc.

Shared knowledge and resources

The brand team is counting on you to bring a wealth of knowledge about key industry trends, and what’s being done on other brands. You could also offer vital advice on whether there are potential solutions already in use. That could be re-purposed for the brand, and also share any useful lessons that were learned on other projects.

Partner with the brand team and the agency

This is a good opportunity to position yourself as a digital partner for the brand, but it's equally important to set the context for the agency. The agency should recognize that they need to work closely with you throughout to deliver the project.

You are very much a member of the working team. Apart from your expertise in digital, you are there to advise the agency on Diageo ways of working, and to uphold Diageo standards and general policies, legal, etc.

4What triggers this phase?

A new digital need.

5Keep your checklist handy

√ If the brand team is launching a new digital initiative, then help them to prepare the PID

√ If the brand team is going through agency selection, then guide them in the process involved to put an agency on the roster; offer your point of view about the selection of the agency – remember you will be working closely with the agency to deliver the digital solution.

√ Help the brand team to prepare a brief for the agency

√ Investigate if there is anything already in the toolbox, or build for other brands that is fit for re-use / re-purpose

√ Share lessons learned from other projects that could be valuable to this project
